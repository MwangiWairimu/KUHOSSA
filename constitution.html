THE CONSTITUTION

Preamble

We the members of Kenyatta University Occupational Health and Safety Students’ Association:

  • Acknowledge the supremacy of the Almighty God in every aspect of this constitution.
  • Are proud of our cultural, ethnicity, and education level diversity and are determined to engage and relate harmoniously and peacefully as guided by this constitution.
  • Recognize the need to have an Association for a good relationship between us and the institution.
  • Adopt and give this constitution to ourselves and our future generations.
  • Commit this constitution as a supreme tool of power guiding each and every member now and in generations to come.

Chapter One: KUOHSSA Identity

Section 01: Name

  1. The association shall be known as the KENYATTA UNIVERSITY OCCUPATIONAL HEALTH AND SAFETY STUDENTS’ASSOCIATION herein also referred to as KUOHSSA, and which shall be duly registered by the dean of the students’ affairs.
  2. KENYATTA UNIVERSITY OCCUPATIONAL HEALTH AND SAFETY STUDENTS’ ASSOCIATION shall for practical and material reasons abbreviated as KUOHSSA.

Section 02: Area of Operation

  1. This constitution shall have the full force of law at every chapter of KUOHSSA.
  2. The head office of KUOHSSA shall be situated at the main campus of the university; any chapter declared to be part of KUOHSSA shall have a representative to the executive committee; provided that such a chapter shall not be within the main campus; and provided that such chapter shall be accountable to KUOHSSA.

Section 03: Aims and Objectives

The aims and objectives of KUOHSSA shall be:
  • To identify and spearhead activities that promote Health and Occupational Safety within and outside the university through methods such as:
    • Conferences and seminars.
    • Occupational health awareness campaigns.
    • Occupational health promotive workshops.
    • Health checkups at individual level.
    • Community service such as clean ups and education to vulnerable groups in the society.
  • Work closely with the directorate of Occupational Health and Safety (DOSH) whose main role is to promote occupational health and safety in the country.
  • To liaise with other clubs and groups within the campus as well as external organizations that are either working for occupational health and safety.
  • To identify research and analyze Occupational Health and Safety issues and suggestion practical solutions to then same.
  • To be a means through which members nurture the qualities of responsibility, leadership, accountability and other virtues as well as talents.

Section 04: Activities

  • In order to achieve the above objectives, the club shall perform the following activities:
    • Participate in seminars, conferences, symposiums that address occupational health issues.
    • Organize public lectures, conferences, seminars workshops, plays, video shows, and other activities which will lead to the achievement of the association’s objectives.
    • Contribute to publications and articles in the press and mass media as a whole which generally promotes Occupational Health and Safety
  • All activities carried out shall lead to the achievement of KUOHSSA objectives and must be consistent to the Kenyatta University rules and regulations and the KUOHSSA constitution.

Section 05: Organs of KUOHSSA

The organs of KUOHSSA shall be:
  • The executive Committee
  • Chapters of KUOHSSA
  • Special committees formed in accordance to the KUOHSSA constitution subject to approval by the executive committee and or members.

Chapter Two: Membership

Section 06: Determination of Membership

  1. Any student admitted in Kenyatta University for a course leading to the qualification of an award of Bachelor of Science in Occupational Health and Safety shall be an ordinary member of KUOHSSA upon paying a membership fee of ksh.100 and a semesterly subscription of ksh.100
  2. Any person, who after completion of his/her studies at the university wishes to join KUOHSSA, shall be admitted as an associate member upon payment of a membership fee of ksh.500 and a semesterly subscription of ksh.500.
  3. Secondary schools, environmental clubs and colleges wishing to join KUOHSSA shall be admitted as affiliate members, upon payment of a membership fee of ksh.500 and a semesterly subscription of ksh.500.
  4. Organizations and institutions shall be admitted as cooperate members upon payment of a membership fee of ksh.1000 and a semesterly subscription of 1000.
  5. Any registered member shall be deemed Bonafede upon payment of all semesterly subscriptions.

Section 07: Cessation of Membership

  1. An ordinary member of KUOHSSA shall cease to be a member if;
    • A disciplinary committee so recommends
    • A general meeting is held and at least two third of the members present thereby resolve that such a member be expelled on the grounds that his/her conduct or character has contravened any provision of the KUOHSSA a constitution.
  2. An associate, cooperate or affiliate member shall cease to be a member of KUOHSSA when such a member ceases to make subscription as provided by the constitution. KUOHSSA.
  3. Any member who ceases to be a member of KUOHSSA shall not be entitled to any refund of subscription fee or any money contributed by him/her any time or to any part thereof.
  4. Any person who shall cease to be a member of KUOHSSA shall be required to surrender all the KUOHSSA property to the executive and cease to participate in KUOHSSA activities.

Section 08: The Disciplinary Committee

  1. The KUOHSSA disciplinary committee shall consist of:
    1. Five ordinary members of whom two shall be from the executive.
    2. The KUOHSSA chairperson shall chair the disciplinary committee unless he/she is subject to the committee.
    3. In the event of (b) above, the executive shall appoint one office bearer to take up the position of the chairperson, and the disciplinary committee shall appoint one member among themselves to chair the committee.
    4. The other executive members shall be the legal affairs secretary provided that he/she is not a subject to the committee.
    5. The other three members shall be non-executive members appointed by the chairperson.
    6. The membership of the disciplinary committee shall be presided by the executive committee and approved by the members.
    7. Appeals reached by the committee shall be directed to the patron of KUOHSSA inwriting.
    8. Further appeals shall be directed to the dean of students in writing but only after (g)

Section 09: Disciplinary Action

  1. Any member who revels the KUOHSSA secrets to unauthorized persons shall be summoned to appear before the disciplinary committee.
  2. Any member of official who misappropriates the KUOHSSA funds shall face the disciplinary committee.
  3. Any member who uses an indecent language during meetings shall be summoned by the executive to appear before the committee.
  4. General disturbances are forbidden during meetings.
  5. Failure to adhere to KUOHSSA policies and regulations shall lead to suspension of a member after one verbal and written waning.
  6. Failure to attend three executive meetings without any advance notification to the secretary general shall lead to suspension for one semester coming into force from the following semester.
  7. Members who shall breach the KUOHSSA guidelines by doing things that are not interest of KUOHSSA welfare shall appear before the disciplinary committee.
  8. Any elected member who fails to carry out their duties will be voted out.
  9. A member can move a motion of no confidence with a support of at least ten signatures of members
  10. Any elected or appointed official alleged or suspected to have acted in a way that meets the threshold of appearing before the disciplinary committee shall step aside immediately to allow for investigations.

Section 10: Rights of Members

  1. Every ordinary member will have a fair and equitable right to access the services and facilities offed by KUOHSSA facility or service
  2. Subject to provisions of this constitution, every member except associate members, affiliate members and corporate members shall have then right to vie for any position declared open for contest.
  3. Every member except associate member, affiliate member, and corporate member shall be entitled to only one vote for each of the positions contested by more than one member at election held and conducted under the provisions of this constitution.
  4. Every member shall be entitled to view the constitution at any given time including a copy of Braille to every blind member.
  5. Every member shall have the right to inspect KUOHSSA books of account after the lapse of 14calendar days written notice to the treasurer.

Section 11: Obligations of Members

  1. Every member shall respect KUOHSSA office bearers including decisions and actions legally made by them as established in this constitution.
  2. Every member shall respect property owned by the KUOHSSA; and any other member who with malice causes damage to property owned by KUOHSSA shall be subject to the disciplinary provisions of KUOHSSA.
  3. Every member shall be required to honor individual financial obligations of KUOHSSA; including and in particular shall pay membership fee at the time of joining KUOHSSA and subscription fee, within the first two weeks of the subsequent semester the member is registered for study at the University, failure to subscribe within the first two weeks a member shall be required to pay a fine of ksh.50;failure to meet the fine shall lead to automatic forfeiture of membership.

Section 12: Registration

  • The registration of new members shall be done through the year representatives who shall then submit the registration list to the secretary general. The registration list and the subscription fee shall also be submitted to the treasurer.

Chapter Three: THE Executive Committee

Section 13:The Composition of the Executive Committee

  • The KUOHSSA executive committee shall consist of the following who shall be elected with the provisions of the constitution;
    1. Chairperson
    2. Vice chairperson
    3. Secretary general
    4. Deputy secretary general
    5. Organizing secretary
    6. Deputy organizing secretary
    7. Deputy organizing secretary
    8. Treasurer
    9. Publicity secretary
    10. Four-year representatives
    11. Campus representative

Section 14:The Executive Authority

  1. The executive authority of KUOHSSA shall rest on the executive committee. The executive shall have such powers and duties executed in a manner as it shall be prescribed in this constitution.
  2. The executive shall be fully responsible for the general administration of KUOHSSA.
  3. The executive committee shall have the authority and responsibility as provide in this constitution and in particular shall:
    1. Ensure that all members uphold the constitution.
    2. Formulate policies and by-laws subject to approval by the club members for proper running of the club and its organs.
    3. To appoint the non-elective members ad-hoc committees.
    4. Make decisions in case of emergence without due consultation provided that such decisions shall be reported to members as soon as possible.
    5. Keep accurate and to date record of KUOHSSA

Section 15:Decision Making

  1. Except as otherwise provided in this constitution, any matter subject to decision making in the executive meeting shall be determined by the majority of voters of the members
  2. On any questions proposed in the executive meetings, the person posing the question shall:
    1. If he is the chairperson, have the casting vote but an original vote.
    2. If he is not, have both the original and casting vote.

Section 16:Qualification for Election to the Executive Committee

  1. A person shall qualify to contest to the executive if such a person is:
    1. A fully paid member of KUOHSSA
    2. A registered member of KUOHSSA
  2. A person shall not be eligible to contest an elective position in KUOHSSA if such a person;
    1. Previously been removed from the office for errors, misconduct or violation of kuohssa constitution.
    2. Has not sent in a written application within the stipulated period as it shall be provided by this constitution and upon invitation of such applications by the electoral board.
    3. Is an associate, affiliate or corporate member.
    4. Is a member of the KUOHSSA electoral board.
    5. Is in his/her final year study at the University.

Section 17:Meetings of the Executive Committee

  1. The executive shall normally meet at least six times during the semester.
  2. Every meeting of the executive shall be convened by the chairperson in a manner resolved by the said committee in its meetings following inauguration of the office.
  3. The quorum for the commencement and conduct of a meeting of the executive committee shall be two-thirds (2/3) of all members
  4. There shall preside at any meeting of the executive committee;
    1. The chairperson; or
    2. In the absence of the chairperson, the deputy chairperson; or
    3. In the absence of the vice chairperson, the secretary general; or
    4. In the absence of the three mentioned above, any other member of the executive committee elected by members at that time.

Section 18:The KUOHSSA Patron

  1. There shall be a patron nominated by the kuohssa executive committee from either the academic or administrative staff and shall be ratified and approved by the club members. The patron shall:
    1. Be the chief advisor of the KUOHSSA and shall not hold any voting power.
    2. Help kuohssa in connection with the executive committee in raising funds.
    3. Act as a link between the executive and the administration as well as the public.
  2. The patron shall hold office for two academic years and shall obey and respect the KUOHSSA constitution.
  3. After two years, the patron can be elected or re-elected as provided in this constitution.

Chapter Four: OFFICE BEARERS

Section 19:The Chairperson

  1. There shall be created the office of the chairperson in the executive meeting.
  2. The chairperson shall have the responsibility and authority as provided in this constitution, and in particular shall;
    1. Preside over executive meetings, annual general meeting and special meetings.
    2. Represent KUOHSSA at national and international levels on issues relevant to KUOHSSA interests and report back to the executive and the club members.
    3. Ensure existence of proper and cordial relationship between various organs of KUOHSSA.
    4. Be a signatory of KUOHSSA bank account.
    5. Take the overall charge of KUOHSSA.
    6. Delegate and or assign duties to any member upon approval by the executive committee.
    7. Sign memoranda of understanding and or agreement with any organization whose interests deem relevant to KUOHSSA upon approval by the executive committee.
    8. Sign all KUOHSSA minutes for circulation after approval by the executive committee.

Section 20:The Deputy Chairperson

  1. There is created the office of the deputy chairperson in the executive committee.
  2. The deputy chairperson shall have the authority and responsibility as provided by this constitution and in particular, shall:
    1. Deputize the chairperson.
    2. In absence of the chairperson, assume and act on the authority and responsibility of the chairperson except being a signatory to the kuohssa bank account.
    3. By the virtue of his office, the deputy chairperson is the leader of the executive business in general meetings.

Section 21:Secretary General

  1. There is created the office of the secretary in the executive committee.
  2. The secretary general shall have the authority and responsibility as provided by this constitution and in particular, shall:
    1. Keep in proper business manner, true and accurate records of the proceedings of every meeting of the executive committee, AGM and SGM.
    2. Maintain and have custody of all correspondences of KUOHSSA.
    3. Keep updated register of all KUOHSSA members.
    4. Be a signatory to the KUOHSSA bank account.
    5. Be in charge of KUOHSSA office administration.
    6. Be the spokesperson of the AGM and SGM.
    7. Chair the kuohssa meetings in the absence of the chairperson and the deputy chairperson.
    8. Be in charge of all external communication.
    9. Preform any other duty as may from time be assigned by the chairperson upon approval by the executive.

Section 22:The Deputy Secretary General

  1. There shall be the office of the deputy secretary in the executive committee.
  2. The deputy secretary general shall have the authority and responsibility as provided by this constitution and in particular, shall:
    1. Deputize the secretary general.
    2. In absence of the secretary general, assume and act on the authority and responsibility of the secretary general.
    3. Preform any other duty assigned by the chairperson upon approval by the executive committee.

Section 23:The Organizing Secretary

  1. There shall be the office of the deputy secretary in the executive committee.
  2. The organizing secretary shall have the authority and responsibility as provided by this constitution and in particular, shall:
    1. Co-ordinate the activities of the executive committee with other organs of KUOHSSA.
    2. Receive and record requests and grievances from members and forward the same to the executive committee for deliberation.
    3. Avail or cause to be availed the facility for the meetings of KUOHSSA
    4. In consultation with, and on the advice of the executive committee, assist to facilitate the orientation of new members.
    5. Be in charge of coordination during KUOHSSA meetings
    6. Be in charge of all internal communications of KUOHSSA
    7. Arrange and organize KUOHSSA social events.
    8. Take charge of all KUOHSSA matters on gender, special needs and other related issues.
    9. Perform any other duty assigned by the chairperson upon approval by the executive committee

Section 24:Deputy Organizing Secretary

  1. There is created the office of the deputy organizing secretary in the executive committee
  2. The organizing secretary shall have the authority and responsibility as provided in this constitution and in particular shall:
    1. Deputize the organizing secretary
    2. In absence of the organizing secretary, assume and act on the authority and responsibility of the organizing secretary.
    3. Perform any other duty assigned by the chairperson upon approval by the executive committee

Section 25:The Treasurer

  1. There is created the office of the treasurer in the executive committee.
  2. The treasurer shall have the authority and responsibility and in the particular as provided by this constitution shall:
    1. Ensure that every payment and expenditure is duly authorized
    2. Be a mandatory signatory to the kuohssa bank account.
    3. Keep petty the groups petty cash as it shall be agreed by the executive committee.
    4. Collect monies for banking from members and year representatives for banking
    5. Ensure that all books of account are safe.
    6. Prepare and present semester budget to the members during the first general meetings upon executive approval.
    7. Perform any other duty as may from time to time be assigned by the chairperson upon approval by the executive committee.

Section 26:Legal Affairs Secretary

  1. There is created the office of the legal affairs secretary in the executive committee.
  2. The legal affairs secretary shall have the authority and responsibility as provided in this constitution and, in particular shall;
    1. Protect and ensure the implementation of the KUOHSSA constitution.
    2. Advise and give guidelines on legal and constitutional matters especially on contentious issues.
    3. Ensure transparency, accountability and equity in the association.
    4. Receiving resignation letters from KUOHSSA executive members.
    5. Advice the chairperson and the executive on signing memoranda agreement and or understanding with any organization whose interest deems relevant to that of KUOHSSA.
    6. Perform any other duty as may from time to time be assigned by the chairperson upon approval by the executive committee.

Section 27:Publicity Secretary

  1. There is created the office of the publicity secretary in the executive committee.
  2. The publicity secretary shall have the authority and responsibility as provided in this constitution and, in particular shall:
    1. Publicize kuohssa both within and outside Kenyatta University.
    2. Ensure that KUOHSSA reputation is maintained.
    3. Facilitate the linking of KUOHSSA and other like-minded external organizations.
    4. Perform any other duty as may from time to time be assigned by the chairperson upon approval by the executive committee.

Section 28:The Year Representatives

  1. There is created the office of the year representatives in the executive committee
  2. The year representatives shall have the authority and responsibility as provided in this constitution and, in particular shall:
    1. Help in updating of mailing list of members from their various years in Kenyatta University.
    2. Disseminate information and decisions from the executive committee to their members.
    3. Help in the registration of new members from their respective years.
    4. Represent the interest of their years to the executive committee.
    5. Help in collection of money from the members and submitting the same to the treasurer for banking.
    6. Perform any other duty as may from time to time be assigned by the chairperson upon approval by the executive committee.

Section 29:Campus Representatives

  1. There is created the office of the campus representatives in the executive committee
  2. The campus representatives shall have the authority and responsibility as provided in this constitution and, in particular shall:
    1. Act as a link between the KUOHSSA head office and their respective campus.
    2. Submit records of their members to the secretary general.
    3. Submit financial records to the treasurer for record keeping.
    4. Register new members from their respective campuses.
    5. Perform any other duty as may from time to time be assigned by the chairperson upon approval by the executive committee.

Section 30:Liability of the Executive

  1. The members of the executive committee may be held jointly and or severally liable for negligence and mismanagement of the affairs of KUOHSSA; but in the event, shall not be liable for misdeeds of the individual members in their private capacity nor suffer victimization in the discharge of their responsibility :provided that they shall carry themselves with responsibly and dignity without misuse of their office.

Section 31:Vacation of Office

  1. A member of the executive committee shall vacate office:
    1. When a new office bearer is elected and inaugurated in accordance with the provisions of this constitution.
    2. If being a member through a vote of no confidence resolve that they have no confidence in such a person being an officer.
    3. When the member fails to attend three consecutive meetings of KUOHSSA without notice.
    4. If such office bearer is convicted of a criminal offence by a competent court of law.
    5. If such a person, by reason of infirmity of mind or body is unable to execute the functions of his office.
    6. If the disciplinary committee resolves, upon approval by the members that such a person vacates office.
    7. If such an office bearer resigns and personally hands in a letter of resignation to the legal affairs secretary, giving reasons for his resignation and such resignation approved by the KUOHSSA members.
  2. In the event a vacancy is created as provided in one (1) above;
    1. If the office left vacant has a deputizing officer, the deputy shall assume and act in the vacancy created until the next general election and the chairperson shall appoint another ordinary member or a member of the executive committee to act and serve in the vacancy after the election of the deputy.
    2. If the vacancy created has no deputy, the executive committee shall appoint a person among the ordinary members to act and serve in the vacancy created until the next general election.
  3. All the offices shall be delegated vacant for contesting after the formation and ratification of the electoral board. However, the sitting office bearers shall remain in their offices until new offices bearers are elected and inaugurated.

Chapter Five: ELECTIONS

Section 31:General Principles of Elections

  1. The electoral system shall be based on the two principles of equality of the vote and the right of every individual to vote and stand to be voted for.
  2. Elections must be free and fair, conducted through a secret ballot and must ensure fair representations of members.
  3. Elections shall be valid if attended by at least 2/3 registered members.
  4. The winner of any particular post must gain the majority votes in the elections.
  5. The candidates wishing to contest for KUOHSSA seats shall submit their applications to the secretary of the electoral board two weeks prior the election date.
  6. The chairperson of the KUOHSSA shall be the returning officer of the electoral board.
  7. The counting of votes shall take place immediately after voting and the results announced at the same date.
  8. Election petition shall be appealed to the KUOHSSA elections board.
  9. In case the contestant is not satisfied with the verdict of the board in (8) above he/she shall make further appeals to the dean students affairs whose ruling shall stand final.

Section 32:KUOHSSA Electoral Board

  1. The kuohssa elections board shall be appointed, constituted and established three weeks prior to the election date to start preparations for the elections.
  2. The board members shall be appointed by the executive committee and ratified by at least two-thirds (2/3) majority of the KUOHSSA members within two calendar days after the appointments
  3. Upon ratification, the board shall begin to receive names and applications of contestants two weeks prior the elections date.
  4. The board shall consist of:
    1. The chairperson who shall preside over as the chairperson of the board.
    2. Two associate members appointed by the executive and ratified by KUOHSSA members as stated in (2) above
    3. Two ordinary members in their final year of study at the university appointed and ratified as by the KUOHSSA members as stated in (2) above.

Section 33:Authority and Responsibility of the KUOHSSA Election Board

  1. The board shall have the authority and responsible of:
    1. Conduct and supervise the elections.
    2. Promote and ensure free and fair elections.
    3. Prepare and avail election materials prior the coting date.
    4. Count and announce the results through the boards chairperson
    5. Receiving application letters from interested members
  2. The board members shall not be eligible for nomination to contest and shall not be eligible to vote.
  3. The board shall give each of the contestants and opportunity to talk to the KUOHSSA members before for the commencement of the voting exercise.

Chapter Six: MEETINGS

Section 34:Categories of Meetings

There shall be four types of meetings;
  1. Annual general meetings.
  2. Executive meetings.
  3. Special general meetings.
  4. Any other meetings as shall be approved by the executive committee.

Section 35:The Annual General Meetings

  1. Shall be held on the twelve week of the second semester each academic year with explicit objective of deliberating on the general KUOHSSA issues.
  2. Notice of such meetings accompanied by the agenda shall be availed to all members not less than three (3) calendar days before the meeting.

Section 36:The Agenda of The Annual General Meetings

The agenda of the annual general meetings shall include:
  1. Confirmation of the previous annual general meeting minutes.
  2. Consideration of the KUOHSSA account.
  3. Submission of the general reports by the sitting officials.
  4. Election and inauguration of new office bearers.
  5. Any other matter as the executed shall deem necessary and as for which a member or members shall have a notice in writing to the secretary at least one week before the meeting.

Chapter Seven: FINANCE AND INVESTMENTS

Section 37:Sources of Finance

The finance of KUOHSSA shall be derived from;
  1. KUOHSSA membership fee.
  2. Revenue earned from investments.
  3. Fundraising activities and grants.
  4. Fines from members.
  5. Any other source of revenue approved by the executive committee.

Section 38:Collection of Revenue

  1. Collection of KUOHSSA fees shall be done and remitted to the KUOHSSA bank account, in a manner prescribed by the university council such that;
    1. Membership fee of ksh.100 as contained in section (6) shall be paid once upon registration for membership at the KUOHSSA.
    2. Semester subscription fee of ksh.100 shall be paid every semester beginning from the semester of registration as KUOHSSA member.
  2. The collection of KUOHSSA revenues shall be conducted by the year and campus representatives under the co-ordination by the treasurer.
  3. Upon collection, the revenue shall be submitted to the treasurer for banking.
